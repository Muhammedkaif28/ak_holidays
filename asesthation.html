Apostille Procedure

Apostille from MEA can be done after authentication from relevant State Government authorities. This legalization is required when documents are to be used in Hague Convention member countries. MEA Apostille is valid for Indian certificates of all types. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Step 1 – Authentication of Documents

All original documents needing MEA apostille must be authenticated by designated authorities in the State/Union Territory of issuance:

  • Educational Documents: Attested by HRD or Education Department with signatory seal and department stamp.
  • Non‑Educational Documents: Attested by Home or General Administration Department similarly.
  • Commercial Documents: Pre-authenticated by Chambers of Commerce with authorized signatory stamp and details. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Step 2 – Apostille of Documents

The Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) then apostilles the authenticated documents and affixes the Apostille sticker on the reverse of the original. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

What Is an Apostille Stamp?

An Apostille is a square-shaped, computer-generated sticker issued by MEA with a unique identification number. It is pasted on the reverse of original documents and provides online verification of authenticity. Once apostilled, the document does not require embassy attestation in Hague Convention countries. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Documents Eligible for Apostille

  • Educational: Degrees, diplomas, transcripts, MBBS, BE, nursing, CA, engineering certificates etc.
  • Personal/Non‑Educational: Birth, marriage, death, divorce certificates, affidavits, power of attorney.
  • Commercial: Export/import invoices, certificates of origin, business documents, etc. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
