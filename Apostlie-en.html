Process Overview

Step 1 – State Authentication

  • Educational Docs: Attested by HRD / Education Department
  • Personal Docs: Attested by Home / General Administration Dept.
  • Commercial Docs: Verified by Chamber of Commerce

Step 2 – MEA Apostille

After state-level validation, the Ministry of External Affairs issues an Apostille sticker with a unique identification number. This Apostille is valid across all Hague Convention countries—no embassy attestation needed.

Processing Time Estimates

  • Commercial documents: usually 8–10 working days (can extend to 2–3 weeks).
  • Educational & personal documents: timelines depend on state HRD/Home Department verification + MEA workload.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an Apostille?

An Apostille is an official sticker issued by MEA that certifies documents for use in Hague Convention countries.

Do I need Embassy Attestation after Apostille?

No. If the country is a member of the Hague Convention, Apostille is sufficient.

Can I submit documents directly to MEA?

No. Since March 2012, only MEA-authorized outsourcing agencies can accept submissions.
