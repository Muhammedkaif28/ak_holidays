What is MEA Attestation?

MEA Attestation refers to the official process of validating documents by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA), Government of India. It is a mandatory step for documents issued in India to be legally recognized both within the country and abroad. Depending on the destination country and purpose, the MEA either affixes an Attestation Stamp or issues an Apostille.

The MEA is the apex governmental body responsible for handling India's international diplomatic affairs. When it comes to document attestation, MEA plays a critical role in verifying the authenticity of educational, personal, and commercial documents. This verification is often required for international travel, employment, education, or business purposes abroad.

Since March 1, 2012, the MEA has outsourced the submission and collection process for attestation and apostille services to authorized service providers. As a result, individuals can no longer directly submit documents at the MEA's CPV Division, Patiala House, New Delhi. Instead, applicants must submit their original documents, along with photocopies and a valid passport copy, to officially designated agencies.

Submission Process

As of March 1, 2012, the MEA has outsourced the submission and collection services for attestation/apostille. Individuals must submit original documents, photocopies, and passport copies through MEA-authorized agencies.

Types of Documents That Require Attestation

Educational Records

  • Degree Certificates, Mark Sheets
  • Diplomas, Transfer Certificates, Nursing Certificates

Personal Documents

  • Birth, Marriage, Death Certificates
  • Divorce Decrees, Police Clearance Certificates

Commercial Documents

  • Power of Attorney, Export & Import Papers
  • Certificate of Incorporation, MOA
  • Invoices and Business Documents

Step-by-Step Attestation Procedure

  1. State-Level Attestation – Verified by respective State Departments.
  2. MEA Attestation – MEA affixes attestation seal or Apostille stamp.
  3. Embassy Attestation (if needed) – For non-Hague Convention countries.

General Attestation vs. Apostille

Applicable Countries Non-Hague Convention countries Hague Convention countries
Verification Stamped by MEA and respective embassies Single Apostille stamp by MEA
